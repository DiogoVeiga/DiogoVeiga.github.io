About me

I am currently a Full Computational Scientist at the Jackson Laboratory. I joined Jacques Banchereau’s lab at the Jackson Laboratory to help developing a groundbreaking research program aimed at the identification of cancer isoforms using hybrid-capture long-read sequencing. Here, I am leading the development of a proteogenomics pipeline to analyze hybrid-capture long-read isoform sequencing, RNA-seq, and proteomics datasets to discover RNA-based neoantigens that can be exploited for immunotherapy.

I have a broad expertise in Bioinformatics and analysis of next-generation sequencing, including WGS, Exome-seq, RNA-seq, ATAC-seq and Pacbio long read SMRT-seq.

I have a passion for analyzing big data, in particular in the field of genomics, using R, Bioconductor, and Tidyverse.
